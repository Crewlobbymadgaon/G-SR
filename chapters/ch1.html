CHAPTER I

PRELIMINARY

1.01
SHORT TITLE AND COMMENCEMENT
  1. (1)
    These rules may be called the Indian Railways (Open Lines) General (Third Amendment) Rules, 2024.
  2. (2)
    They shall come into force on the date of their publication in the Official Gazette.
1.02
DEFINITIONS — In these rules, unless the context otherwise requires —
  1. (1)
    “Act” means The Railways Act, 1989 (24 of 1989);
  2. (2)
    “Adequate distance” means the distance sufficient to ensure safety;
  3. (3)
    “approach lighting” means an arrangement in which the lighting of signals is controlled automatically by the approach of a train;
  4. (4)
    “approved special instructions” means special instructions approved of or prescribed by the Commissioner of Railway Safety.
  5. (5)
    “authorized officer” means the person who is duly empowered by general or special order of the Railway Administration, either by name or by virtue of his office, to issue instructions or to do any other things,
S.R.1.02(5)(1)
The Chief Operating Manager is the “Authorized Officer” for the Konkan Railway Corporation.
  1. (6)
    “authority to proceed” means the authority given to the Loco Pilot of a train, under the system of working, to enter the block section with his train.
  2. (7)
    “axle counter” means an electrical device which, when provided at two given points on the track, proves by counting axles in and counting axles out, whether the section of the track between the said two points is clear or occupied;
  3. (8)
    “block back” means to despatch a message from a block station intimating to the block station immediately in rear on a double line, or to the next block station on the either side on a single line, that the block section is obstructed or is to be obstructed;
  4. (9)
    “block forward” means to despatch a message from a block station on a double line intimating to the block station immediately in advance the fact that the block section in advance is obstructed or is to be obstructed.
  5. (10)
    “block section” means that portion of the running line between two block stations on to which no running train may enter until Line Clear has been received from the block station at the other end of the block section;
  6. (11)
    “Centralized Traffic Control” means a system by which the working of trains over a route, to which the system applies, is governed by fixed signals remotely controlled from a designated place;
  7. (12)
    “Centralized Traffic Control Operator” means the person on duty who may, for the time being, be responsible for the working of trains on the Centralized Traffic Control;
  8. (13)
    “Commissioner of Railway Safety” means an inspector appointed to exercise any functions under the Act, and includes an Additional Commissioner of Railway Safety;
  9. (14)
    “competent railway servant” means a railway servant duly qualified to under take and perform the duties entrusted to him;
  10. (15)
    “connections” when used with reference to a running line, means the points and crossings or other appliances used to connect such line with other lines or to cross it;
  11. (16)
    “Controller” means a railway servant on duty who may for the time being be responsible for regulating the working of traffic on a section of a railway provided with the system of speech communication;
  12. (17)
    “day” means from sunrise to sunset;
  13. (18)
    “direction of traffic” means —
    (a) on a double line, the direction for which the line is signaled; (b) on a single line, the direction for the time being established, under the system of working, to allow trains to move in that direction;
  14. (19)
    “Loco Pilot” means the engine driver or any other competent railway servant for the time being in charge of driving a train;
  15. (20)
    “electrical communication instrument” means either a telephone or a Morse telegraph instrument;
  16. (21)
    “facing and trailing points”: points are facing or trailing in accordance with the direction a train or vehicle moves over them. Points are said to be facing points when by their operation a train approaching them can be directly diverted from the line upon which it is running;
  17. (22)
    “fixed signal” means a signal of fixed location indicating a condition affecting the movement of a train and includes a semaphore arm or disc or fixed light for use by day and fixed light for use by night;
S.R.1.02(22)(1)
Colour light signal — A colour light signal is also a fixed signal. A colour light signal is a signal which shows coloured light (or lights) both by day and night.
  1. (23)
    “fouling mark” means the mark at which the infringement of fixed Standard Dimensions occurs, where two lines cross or join one another;
  2. (24)
    “Gangman” means a railway servant employed on permanent way or work connected therewith;
S.R.1.02(24)(1)
“Track Maintainer” means a railway servant employed on permanent way or work connected therewith:
Note: Henceforth wherever the designation mentioned as “Track Safety Man” shall be read as “Track Maintainer performing the duties of Track safety man”.
  1. (25)
    “Gangmate” means the person in charge of a gang of workmen employed on permanent way or work connected therewith;
  2. (26)
    “Gateman” means a competent railway servant posted at a level crossing for working the gates;
  3. (27)
    “Goods train” means a train (other than a material train) intended solely or mainly for the carriage of animals or goods;
